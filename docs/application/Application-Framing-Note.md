# Application Framing Note

I created the attached playbook because I believe Codex deployment is not just a tooling rollout. It is an SDLC transformation problem.

The teams that get the most value from Codex will not be the teams that simply tell engineers to "use AI more." They will be the teams that identify specific engineering workflows where Codex can reduce ambiguity, speed up implementation, improve review quality, expand test coverage, and strengthen operational readiness without weakening human ownership.

This playbook shows how I would approach that work with a customer engineering team:

- Start with real SDLC friction.
- Turn ambiguous specs into implementation-ready plans.
- Use Codex for scoped feature work, refactors, and bug isolation.
- Apply Codex to PR review, test generation, and release readiness.
- Build reusable prompts, demos, workshops, and reference workflows.
- Capture customer deployment patterns as product feedback.

My background is directly aligned with this motion. I have worked as a customer-facing technical strategist and AI workflow builder, translating ambiguous business and technical problems into working demos, structured systems, and adoption-ready workflows. I have built with Codex, Cursor, ClaudeCode, React, Next.js, Supabase, RAG patterns, and voice AI, and I am especially interested in helping engineering teams turn AI-assisted development from experimentation into disciplined operating practice.

The attached artifact is meant to show the kind of customer-facing technical thinking I would bring to the AI Deployment Engineer, Codex role: practical, specific, safety-conscious, and focused on making Codex useful inside real engineering organizations.
